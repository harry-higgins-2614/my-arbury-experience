My Experience with Arbury Motor Group

A personal account of a vehicle purchase and warranty issue involving a Vauxhall Zafira Tourer (Reg: ML18 FWP)

The following account can be interpreted as my review of Arbury.

Introduction

In February 2024, I purchased a used Vauxhall Zafira Tourer from Arbury Motor Group in Walsall. Alongside the vehicle, I was sold an extended warranty (SPOTiCAR Extended Warranty), which was presented as offering peace of mind for unexpected major repair costs.

This website documents my experience since that purchase — including significant mechanical issues, poor communication, and my attempts to resolve the situation fairly through Arbury.

Timeline of Events

  • February 2024: Vehicle purchased from Arbury Walsall with SPOTiCAR Extended Warranty. Mileage at purchase: 59,050.
  • April 2025: Engine Management Light appears. Car begins stalling from neutral and running rough. Garage advises it is unsafe to drive and diagnoses suspected timing chain and clutch issues (~£2,100).
  • 10 April 2025: I contact Arbury to begin the warranty process.
  • 14 April – 30 April 2025: Multiple follow-ups with little to no response.
  • May 2025: Arbury advises they can look at the car but will not cover recovery. I am informed diagnostics are free for up to one hour only — any further time will be chargeable at a rate of £180/hr.

Concerns Raised

  • Lack of meaningful communication for weeks despite a serious issue.
  • No support offered for vehicle recovery despite the car being undrivable.
  • Potential for high diagnostic costs — even though I’ve already paid for a third-party diagnostic.
  • Warranty sold as peace of mind but now creating financial uncertainty before support is even confirmed.

What I’m Asking For

  • That Arbury covers the cost of recovery, or arranges it without charge, given the condition and circumstances.
  • That no diagnostic charges are passed to me, whether the repair is deemed valid under warranty or not.

Disclaimer
